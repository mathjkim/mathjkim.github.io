Configuration spaces of repulsive particles
on a metric graph

For my dissertaion research, I constructed a discrete model that captures the homotopy type of the topological configuration spaces of repulsive particles on a tree, and computed the upper bound to the dimension of non-trivial homology. Let me explain the problem I worked on with an example.

Imagine robots working in a factory. When planning motions of the robots, we need to the configuration space of the robots. One might asks:

(1) Can the robots always switch their positions one another without collision? (=Is it connected?)
(2) How does the configuration space look like? Is there a hole in the configuration space?

Topology is a mathematical tool to answer these types of questions. Of course, we need a more specific question to find an answer. Just like you want to refine your question in ChatGPT)
